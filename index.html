ChampBot-2015

A Champ-like ROV, with brief submersion capability, designed to meet the demands of the Champlain Mini Maker Faire's ChampBot Challenge of 2015

Download Download View ChampBot-2015 on GitHub

Looking part Tanystropheus, part Gungan and sporting a wicked pair of drive-units on a pivoting boom, this bot is nearing completion.

The head is made of a license plate and the eyes are made of ping-pong balls lit by red LEDs. There are parts from several appliances, including a dehumidifier and computer-monitor.

The design is far from any boat or sub, having more in common with a compact, mobile buoy. It would be difficult to have less consideration for hydrodynamics.

Specifications

  • Launched on September 12, 2015 in Arrowhead Lake, East Georgia Vt. (It Floats!)
  • Type: Competition ROV, ChampBot

Key Features

  • Dual Hull, Over/Under, joined by 4 stanchions
  • Dual Drive Units which support piruett turning
  • Pivoting drive boom (propulsion/rotary-dive-plane selection)
  • Jaw control

Payload

  • 1x float, 20 cm from water surface

Dimensions

  • Length: 96 cm
  • Beam: 70 cm
  • Draft: 30 cm (Flank), 40 cm (stopped)
  • Height: 83 cm
  • Displacement Weight 9.2 kg

Main Propulsion

  • 2x Johnson Electric motors with B&D reduction gears and chucks
  • Power: 0.6 kW (0.8 HP) (estimated)
  • Propellers: 2x, 23 cm diameter, 10 cm pitch
  • Max Speed: 3 kph (1.62 knots) (estimated)

Power

  • 5 Ah, 12.6 V AGM
    • 30 Amp, self-resetting breaker

Armament

  • 1x 90+ kW PFI (Projected Flame Igniter)
    • Butane with water-heated evaporator
    • Multi-spark, capacitive discharge pre-ignitor
    • 5 mm burner with wind guard
    • 40 second capacity (3.6 MJ)
